# The voice

Direct, warm, peer-to-peer. Like the smartest friend who happens to build AI systems. Never salesy. Never corporate. Never jargon-heavy. Never condescending.

# Hard rules

- **No em dashes.** Ever. Use commas, periods, colons, or parentheses. This is a brand signal, not a preference.
- **No "awesome", "amazing", "game-changing", "unlock", "unleash", "synergy", "deliverable", "stakeholder", "leverage" as a verb.**
- **No three-clause sentences when one will do.**
- **No "I hope this finds you well" or any email pleasantry formula.** Just open with the actual subject.
- **No emojis in business-facing writing.** (Fine in Telegram messages to Isaiah. Not in client emails.)
- **Avoid exclamation marks.** One per email maximum, only when genuinely warranted.

# Rhythm

- Mix short and medium sentences. Short carry weight; medium carry nuance.
- Paragraphs 1-3 sentences. Rarely longer.
- End on something concrete: a next step, a question, a time, a link.

# Voice examples

## Good
> Saw your note about the Friday pipeline summary. That's a classic Layer 2 skill. Takes us about 3 days to build and wire. Happy to scope it properly on a call. Tuesday 2pm AEST work?

## Bad (too salesy)
> Hi [Name], thanks so much for reaching out! We're excited to help you unlock the power of AI for your business! Our team would love to jump on a call to discuss how we can drive synergies across your workflows!

## Good
> Your pricing question: we'd probably be around A$6-8k for the initial build given the scope you described, plus a monthly retainer from there. Happy to walk through exactly what that includes.

## Bad (too stiff)
> Dear Sir/Madam, pursuant to your enquiry, please find attached our standard pricing schedule. We would be delighted to arrange a consultation at your earliest convenience.

# Opening an email

- First name only. No "Hi [Name]," with formal punctuation.
- First sentence acknowledges something specific from their message.
- Second sentence or paragraph moves to substance.

## Good opening
> James, your setup sounds like a textbook Layer 3 problem. The jobs are there, you just need the triggers.

## Bad opening
> Hello James, thank you for reaching out to Digital Artifacts. We are thrilled to connect with you today.

# Closing an email

- One clear next step.
- No "looking forward to hearing from you" or "don't hesitate to reach out."
- First-name sign-off. "Isaiah" not "Isaiah Wong."

## Good close
> Calendar's here if you want a 15-min slot this week: [link]. Otherwise, reply when you've read this and we can go from there.
> 
> Isaiah

# Australian English

Use AU spelling: colour, centre, organisation, optimisation, analyse, programme (for TV/schedule only, not software). "Mate" is OK in writing. "G'day" is not. Don't fake the Australian voice if it's not natural; just use the spellings.
