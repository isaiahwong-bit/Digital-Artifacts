# Primary ICP

Australian small-to-mid business owner (5-50 staff) who is:
- Drowning in repetitive ops work that can't scale without hiring
- Already paying for ChatGPT / Claude / similar, but frustrated by the drift, the hallucinations, and the lack of audit trail
- Technical enough to recognise n8n / Git / Slack but does not want to build the plumbing themselves
- Sold on AI in principle, unconvinced by single-prompt tools in practice

# Three industry archetypes

## A. Professional services firm
- Law firms, accounting practices, consultancies, agencies
- 5-25 fee-earners
- Pain: proposal drafting, meeting prep, matter briefings, pipeline visibility for partners
- They spend Friday afternoon writing updates that should have been drafted themselves
- Decision makers: managing partner, practice manager, operations lead
- Budget signal: happy spending $2-5k/mo on Ops, but hires instead because they can't find the right automation partner

## B. Trades / field services
- Plumbing, electrical, building, HVAC, pest control, cleaning
- 3-20 crew
- Pain: quoting, scheduling, invoice chasing, job wrap-ups, lead intake from multiple channels
- Office staff is one part-timer who is always drowning
- Decision makers: owner-operator (usually on the tools half the week)
- Budget signal: allergic to SaaS subscriptions, will spend on "gets jobs done"

## C. B2B sales team
- Agencies, SaaS, consultancies selling to other businesses
- 2-15 sales/BD headcount
- Pain: lead triage, CRM hygiene, discovery-call logging, pipeline risk flagging, competitor battlecard upkeep
- Reps want to sell; admin eats 30% of their week
- Decision makers: head of sales, founder, RevOps lead if one exists
- Budget signal: already paying for HubSpot / Salesforce, willing to layer automation on top

# Qualification signals

## Hot (respond fast, lead the meeting)
- Mentions specific tools they already use (HubSpot, Slack, n8n, Airtable, Xero)
- Describes a specific job that's eating time ("every Friday we...")
- Gives a budget range >$2k in the form
- From one of the three industry archetypes above
- Referral from a known connection

## Warm (respond thoughtfully, ask one good question)
- Interested but vague ("want to automate my business")
- Budget "not sure yet" but otherwise looks real
- Industry adjacent to ICP (health services, non-profit, property)
- Direct Google search (vs social or unknown)

## Cool (respond politely, point to the Blueprint)
- Solo freelancer with no team, unlikely to be serving clients at 4-layer scale
- Explicit ask for a cheap website only
- Budget under $1k
- Looks like a competitor sniffing

# What we will NOT take on

- Industries we have no context for (healthcare / medical with regulatory complexity, education)
- Pure content-generation farms
- Projects where the client wants to be the one writing the prompts from day one
- Hourly consulting without a scoped build
