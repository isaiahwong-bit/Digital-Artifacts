The strategy and the exact thing we give away. Read by `cold-opener-draft` and `teardown-writer`. The shape: give one genuinely useful thing nearly free, no pitch attached, so the value lands before any ask. Trust is earned by giving first, and by the giveaway being good enough to prove we can do the paid thing.

# The salt (the cold opener)

Not a pitch. One short email that:
- Names a specific repetitive task that business's role almost certainly deals with weekly
- Offers the free teardown, framed as no pitch, no call required, just reply with the task
- Asks one low-effort thing: reply with the one task eating the most time

The salt makes them thirsty. It does not describe Digital Artifacts, the 4 layers, pricing, or ask for a meeting. The only call to action is "reply with the task."

# The pretzel (the free teardown)

When they reply with a task, we send back a one-page sketch of how a 4-layer system would take that task off their plate. This is the giveaway and the demo at the same time. It must be:

- **Specific to their task**, not a template. If it could have been sent to anyone, it failed.
- **Honest.** If the task is not worth automating, say so and why. That single act of honesty is worth more than the sketch itself, because it makes the paid recommendation believable.
- **Concrete.** Which layer does what, roughly what it takes to build, roughly what it saves. Real numbers where defensible, ranges where not.
- **Free of jargon walls.** They do not need to learn the framework to read it. The framework is how we think, not what they have to digest.

# The upsell (earned, not cold)

The teardown ends with one soft next step: a 15-minute call to scope the build, set up by replying to the email. No urgency, no discount, no follow-up pressure in the same email. Value has been delivered, so the ask is allowed to be light.

# What stays free vs paid

- **Free:** the qualifier read, the cold opener, one personalised teardown, one reply to a clarifying question.
- **Paid:** building the thing. Scoping call, then a fixed-price 4-layer deploy per `pricing.md`.

If a prospect tries to extract a second and third teardown without engaging, that is fine once, then the reply-classifier flags it and the next reply points to the booking link rather than another free sketch.

# The meta-proof

Every email in this sequence was drafted by the same kind of system we are offering to build. We do not say this in the opener (it reads as a gimmick cold). We can say it in the teardown or on the call, because by then they have felt it work rather than been told.

# Tone

All of `tone-of-voice.md` applies. No em dashes. No pleasantries. No "I hope this email finds you well." Plain, direct, Australian. First-name sign-off, Isaiah. The opener is shorter than feels comfortable. Brevity is part of the gift.
