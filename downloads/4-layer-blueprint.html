/ The 4-Layer Blueprint
A framework for building AI that actually works

Four layers.
One operating system
for your business.

Most AI tools today are a single prompt box. They forget, hallucinate, and nobody can audit them. The 4-layer blueprint splits that prompt into four clean parts so each one is inspectable, fixable, and portable across models. This is the framework we use on every build.

01
Context / the brain
Your business, written down. Positioning, clients, pipeline, SOPs, tone. Structured markdown in a Git repo. Human-editable, AI-readable, version-controlled forever.
02
Skills / the how
Reusable recipes for the jobs your team does every week. Each skill knows which parts of Layer 1 to read and what to produce. Named. Versioned. Composable.
03
Automation / the triggers
Scheduled and event-driven n8n workflows that wire Context and Skills to the rest of your stack. Cron jobs, webhooks, API calls, conditional branches, retries.
04
Agent / the face
Slack, SMS, voice, dashboards. How your team talks to the system and the system talks back. One brain, many surfaces.
Governing rule
Humans write. AI reads. The brain is only edited through review. No drift, no hallucinated history.

/ Worksheet

Map your own stack.

For each layer, jot down what your business already has and what's missing. Don't write the perfect version. Write the honest one. 20 minutes with this page tells you where to start.

Context 01

What does your team already "just know" that should be written down?

Examples client briefs, pricing logic, SOPs, tone rules, competitor notes, pipeline stages, team roster, non-negotiables.
Skills 02

Which repeatable jobs does your team do every week?

Examples draft a proposal, prep for a meeting, triage inbound, write a follow-up, log a discovery call, build a quote.
Automation 03

What could start itself? Cron, webhook, inbound event?

Examples 8:30am daily brief, new lead webhook, deal stuck 14 days, meeting transcript ready, invoice overdue 7 days.
Agent 04

Where does your team actually work? That's the first surface.

Examples Slack DM, team channel, SMS to crew, voice call, email digest, web dashboard, inline HubSpot note.
Next step
Pick one job from Layer 02. Wire it end-to-end first.

Resist the urge to build everything at once. One skill, wired to one trigger, surfaced in one place. Prove it works for a week. Then extend. That's how the brain earns your team's trust.
